# Stuff I Use

Welcome to my `/uses` page! Inspired by folks like [Wes Bos](https://wesbos.com/uses/) and [Kent C. Dodds](https://kentcdodds.com/uses/), this page acts as a living document of my daily workflow. Over the years, I've gradually transitioned toward open-source, open-protocol, and privacy-respecting alternatives wherever possible. Below is the hardware, software, and everyday carry that I rely on as a developer advocate and coffee enthusiast.

Am I missing something that you'd like to know? [Hit me up on BlueSky](https://bsky.app/profile/ryzokuken.dev) and let me know.
Make sure to check out [uses.tech](https://uses.tech) for a list of everyone's `/uses` pages!

> **OS & Hardware Philosophy:** I use bleeding-edge Arch Linux at work because I prefer a snappy, highly-configurable Linux setup for programming. For typing and coding, the tactile feedback of the Halo Trues on my Drop Ctrl keyboard is unmatched.
>
> **The Editor Switch:** I was a long-time user of VSCode Insiders, but I recently switched fully to Zed. Zed is faster, leaner, and gets everything done out of the box without needing a mountain of extensions. Furthermore, VSCode's open core policy keeps getting worse, so moving to something with a better trajectory felt right.

>
> **Coffee Routine:** I use the Sage Bambino for pulling espresso shots daily. I still use my trusty Hario Skerton hand-grinder when I feel like manually cranking the beans, otherwise I fall back to my automatic electric conical burr grinder for convenience.
